Muzhe Yang is the MacFarlane Professor of [Economics](https://business.lehigh.edu/departments/economics) in the [College of Business](https://business.lehigh.edu/directory/muzhe-yang) at [Lehigh University](https://www2.lehigh.edu). His research focuses on identifying factors that contribute to adverse outcomes among vulnerable populations, such as infants and pregnant women. In his empirical studies, he examines a range of environmental factors affecting maternal and infant health, including power plant emissions, various forms of pollution (air, noise, light, and water), as well as factors such as long commutes and working during pregnancy.

Moreover, his work extends to exploring causal relationships with significant policy implications. His research subjects include peer effects in physicians' prescription behaviors for new drugs, the impact of publicly reported provider quality information on the CABG market, the effects of food advertising exposure on purchasing behaviors, the roles of nationality and ethnicity in shaping international and inter-regional trade, the influence of signaling behaviors on college admissions, the impact of paid maternity leave on breastfeeding practices, the mental health implications of the constitutional right to abortion, and the health consequences of circadian misalignment.

Muzhe Yang's research has been mentioned in media outlets such as *USA Today*, *The New York Times*, and *The Washington Post*. For more detailed information, please refer to the Selected Media Mentions section in his [CV](https://muzheyang.github.io/muzhe-yang/).
